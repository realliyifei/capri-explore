Advancements in deep learning models have significantly improved biomedical coreference resolution by integrating domain-specific knowledge and leveraging sophisticated language representation techniques. In recent years, there has been a notable shift from traditional machine learning-based approaches to more advanced deep learning models, which include pre-trained embeddings and specific biomedical features [1] [2].

The integration of biomedical-specific information has allowed these models to more effectively resolve coreferences, a critical task in accurately understanding and extracting information from biomedical texts. Coreference resolution involves identifying when different expressions refer to the same entities, which can be particularly challenging in the biomedical domain due to the complexity and variety of terminology used [5]. By employing deep learning techniques, researchers are able to utilize comprehensive datasets that encapsulate the specific language and relationships inherent to biomedical literature, thereby enhancing the models' understanding of context [3].

The outcomes of these advancements are promising. Studies indicate that deep learning models, particularly those that include knowledge from pre-training on biomedical texts or that incorporate biomedical knowledge bases, substantially improve the performance of coreference resolution tasks. For instance, experiments conducted as part of these studies have shown that such enhancements can effectively capture the nuances of biomedical texts, leading to better identification of coreference links and thus facilitating more accurate information extraction [3] [4].

Moreover, the advancements have fostered a greater focus on the challenges specific to biomedical coreference resolution, which is crucial for supporting downstream tasks, such as information extraction and question answering. The recognition of coreference resolution as a fundamental yet challenging task highlights the importance of ongoing research and development in this area, as it plays a vital role in improving the extraction of complex biomedical information [5]. The combination of sophisticated models and thorough evaluation of biomedical language representations promises to further refine coreference resolution capabilities in the biomedical field, addressing existing limitations and expanding the potential for biomedical text analysis [1] [2].

In conclusion, the evolution towards deep learning models marked a significant milestone in biomedical coreference resolution, leading to more effective and nuanced understanding of biomedical texts. Future research and advancements in this area are expected to continue yielding better tools and methods, ultimately enhancing how biomedical literature can be processed and understood.
